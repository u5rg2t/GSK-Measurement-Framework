              As a follow up to the activity, we'd also like to understand:

              • Retention - How much of the content shared really connected and was retained by the attendees?
              • Relevance - What did people think? Was it a good use of their time?
              • Pulse check - Trying to get a sense of how people are feeling. Are they optimistic or pessimistic?
              • Motivation - Are the attendees motivated by what they heard?

              We know it's difficult to know exactly how many people attended, but give us a sense. Live meetings and broadcast meetings can be particularly difficult but if we could rely on you to use your proessional judgement to think through roughly what the attendace is, that is all we're after.

              The Measurement Framework

              Welcome ICLT!

              This measurement framework has been created help us measure bigger, more complex outcomes than simply looking data and how our specific tactics have performed. This day-to-day data is still important, and we'll certainly want to capture it but this framework aims to simplify the things we measure and ensures we do it consistently.

              Following this framework will allow us to:

              • Have visibility on the activities we're all delivering at various levels in the organisation
              • Make it easy to compare our activity with other locations and business units
              • Understand how this activity all rolls up to contribute to our priority outcomes (currently engagement measures)

              If you have any questions about this framework or how we plan on using it, please contact your local measurement champion within each business unit.
